Organizational Profile

Manolayam Health Care Trust is dedicated to uplifting the lives of the most vulnerable in Needamangalam block, Thiruvarur district. Our services focus on socio-economic empowerment and peaceful living for women, children, the physically challenged, mentally retarded, and elderly.

We provide comprehensive relief and rehabilitation services, including special education, early intervention, community-based support, and assistive device distribution. By empowering individuals and providing access to government benefits, we strive to improve their quality of life.

Our Mission

> Empowering communities through training, guidance, and counseling to enhance their social and economic positions.

> Fostering self-help groups among marginalized and disabled individuals to promote collective empowerment.

> Supporting marginalized groups like women-headed households and HIV+ individuals through tailored training, counseling, and financial assistance.

> Establishing community-based organizations to deliver comprehensive social services.

> Reaching underserved populations through dedicated efforts and innovative strategies.

> Adopting an integrated, multi-sectoral approach to drive sustainable development.

> Providing specialized training, counseling, and health education to empower individuals and communities.

> Promoting equitable access to resources and services through collaborative partnerships and campaigns.

> Improving health outcomes through education, early diagnosis, treatment, and holistic well-being practices.

> Leveraging scientific research and expert knowledge to inform and enhance program effectiveness.

> Bridging gaps in government schemes by promoting awareness, accessibility, and utilization.

> Inspiring and empowering youth and women to overcome challenges and achieve their full potential.

Our Vision

To create an inclusive society where individuals with disabilities can live with dignity and purpose, and where all people have access to quality education, healthcare, and economic opportunities, while preserving the environment for future generations.

Philosophy

“Manolayam Health Care Trust” believes that investment in Human Capital leads to Nation Building

Achievements

> The comprehensive diabetes awareness programs reached over 2000 individuals.

> The massive dengue van awareness campaigns reached 15000 individuals.

> The observation of World Disability Day, Independence Day and Republic Day reaches 10000 population for commitment to empowering individuals with disabilities.

> Under Tamil Nadu Chief Minister Breakfast Scheme, providing daily breakfast to 5 government primary school for 350 students classes 1 to 5,

> The environmental awareness campaign on Polythene Awareness created awareness among 4000 families and now adopting eco-friendly bags,

> The Free Tuition for Children benefited 450 students in classes 4 to 8,

> The Road Safety Program reaches 10,000 public and help to reduce the risk of road accidents.

> We formed above 240 women self help groups and linked credit value for Rs. 12 crore and enhance the economic development,

> The Child Rights Awareness programs raised awareness about child labor and ensuring a child labour free society,

> The HIV/AIDS Awareness and support initiatives empowered 110 vulnerable individuals, particularly women living with HIV,

> The Mask Distribution prevented COVID-19 incidents and safeguard the health of community.

> The Vocational Skill Training on Tailoring equipped 320 young women and secured employment,

> Our Cultural Program on Financial Literacy equips 3000 population and through the Tree Plantation planted 5000 saplings,

> Offered training regularly on Organic Cultivation for 100 farmers,

Future Programs

> The organization plans to establish its own old age home with comprehensive facilities.

> The organization aims to establish a special school for visually impaired, hearing impaired, and orphaned individuals, providing them with all necessary facilities.

> The organization intends to support medical treatment and provide essential tools for visually impaired and hearing-impaired individuals.

> The organization plans to establish a workshop for single women to enable them to earn income.

> The organization aims to establish a corpus fund to support the construction of homes for disadvantaged people.

Our Team

The organization is governed by a 7-member Board of Trustees elected every two years by the General Body. It employs 3 administrative staff and 43 program staff to implement various projects. Additionally, 30 dedicated volunteers support the organization's initiatives.
